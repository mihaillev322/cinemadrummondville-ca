How we organize information (and what we avoid)

Our site is built with simplicity and accessibility at its core. We use no JavaScript for interactive features—only for structured data markup—and no images, which means pages load almost instantly even on slower connections. This design choice also ensures compatibility with assistive technologies, text-only browsers, and privacy-focused configurations.

We rely on semantic HTML5 elements like <section>, <article>, <aside>, and <nav> to give every piece of content clear meaning. Tables summarize comparisons—such as ticket formats or planning timelines—with proper <caption> and <th> elements, so screen readers announce headers correctly. On our FAQ page, we use native <details> and <summary> elements to create collapsible accordions without scripting, keeping the experience lightweight and keyboard-navigable.

Our CSS follows a mobile-first, editorial design philosophy. We use CSS Grid for main layout columns and Flexbox for utility rows, ensuring content reflows gracefully on phones, tablets, and desktops. Typography scales fluidly with clamp(), and we respect prefers-reduced-motion by disabling transitions for users who need calmer interfaces. Focus indicators are bold and high-contrast, meeting WCAG accessibility standards.

We avoid pop-ups, auto-playing media, tracking scripts, and third-party embeds. Every external link opens in a new tab with rel="external noopener" for security and clarity. This minimalist approach means you get the information you came for—no distractions, no data harvesting, no performance penalties.
